autobotx Manual

Welcome to autobotx. This documentation explains how to interact with the system using your voice and hands.

Voice Assistant (J.A.R.V.I.S.)

The voice assistant runs entirely in the background, listening locally for its wake word. Only your explicit commands are processed by the cloud.

  • Wake Word: Say Hey Jarvis or Jarvis to wake the system. Wait for the activation chime.
  • Issue Command: Immediately speak your command after the chime. You have a 10-second window to speak.
  • Auto-Sleep: The system automatically detects silence. Once you stop speaking, it will go back to sleep and fetch your answer.

Context-Aware Spatial AI

AutobotX is a multi-modal assistant. It doesn't just listen; it sees and remembers.

  • Multi-Modal Vision: Hold an object, handwritten note, or code up to the camera and ask Jarvis a question about it. It takes a high-res snapshot and answers intelligently.
  • Desktop Reminders: Ask Jarvis to remind you of something (e.g., "Remind me to drink water in 30 seconds"). It will trigger a native OS Push Notification when the time is up!
  • Empathetic Emotion Engine: Jarvis constantly tracks 52 micro-muscle movements in your face to determine your mood, adapting its conversational tone to match if you are happy, stressed, or surprised.

Hand Tracking & Gestures

The application tracks 21 distinct 3D landmarks on your hands in real-time, functioning completely offline.

  • Tracking: Hold your hand up to the camera. The system will render a precise skeletal overlay on your hand.
  • Sign Language: Perform specific gestures (such as holding up a V sign, pointing, or making a fist) to trigger different output heuristics.
  • No Calibration: The engine will automatically detect whether you are using your left or right hand and adapt the rendering.

Privacy Guarantee

Your continuous camera feed never leaves your device. Constant visual processing (hands, faces, objects) is handled securely in your browser using WebAssembly. The only data transmitted over the internet is a single snapshot sent to the LLM precisely when you trigger a voice command.
